# Dialogues

## First Meeting (Very Easy)

```sentence
アリス：はじめまして。お名前は？
Alice: It is the first time (we meet). What’s your name?
```

```sentence
マサル：僕はマサルと言います。あなたは？
Masaru: I’m Masaru. And you?
```

```sentence
アリス：私はアリスです。1 はじめまして。
Alice: I’m Alice. Nice to meet you.
```

When introducing your own name, you can use either the AはBです or AはBと言います structure.
AはBです means “A is B”, as in “I’m Masaru”. AはBと言います means “I am called Masaru (My name is
Masaru).” AはBです sounds more casual, while AはBと言います is more formal. You can also say
AはBと申します for even greater formality.

```sentence
マサル：アリスさんは学生ですか？
Masaru: Alice, are you a student?
```

```sentence
アリス：はい、私は語学学校で勉強しています。マサルさんは？
Alice: Yes, I’m studying at a language school. What about you, Masaru?
```

```sentence
マサル：僕は働いています。お国はどちらですか？
Masaru: I’m working. Which country are you from?
```

```sentence
アリス：私はイギリスから来ました。
Alice: I’m from the UK.
```

```sentence
マサル：へえ、ロンドン出身ですか？
Masaru: Oh, are you from London?
```

```sentence
アリス：いえ、ロンドンじゃなくて、バーミンガムです。
Alice: No, not London, Birmingham.
```

```sentence
マサル：そうなんですか。いつ日本に来ましたか？
Masaru: I see. When did you come to Japan?
```

```sentence
アリス：去年来ました。
Alice: I came last year.
```

```sentence
マサル：へえ。あのー、連絡先交換できませんか？
Masaru: I see. Um… could we exchange contact information?
```

```sentence
アリス：いいですよ。私のLINEのQRコードを出しますね。
Alice: Sure. I’ll show you my LINE QR code.
```

```sentence
マサル：スキャンしました。今度いっしょに食事でも行きましょう。
Masaru: I scanned it. Let’s go have some meal together sometime.
```

```sentence
アリス：いいですね。私の彼氏、日本人の友達を欲しがっていたので、2よかったです。3
Alice: Sounds good. My boyfriend has been wanting Japanese friends, so this is great.
```

〜がっている is a tricky grammar point. If you are a beginner, please skip this explanation
and just learn the phrase as it is. This grammar is used to describe someone’s outward
behavior that expresses their inner feelings. 〜がっている is the teiru-form of 〜がる, which
basically turns an i-adjective into a verb. For example, if you add がる to 痛い (hurts), it
will become 痛がる, which refers to the act of expressing the inner feeling of pain. While
playing soccer, a ball struck the boy in the groin, and he doubled over in agony,
clutching himself and crying out, “Ahhh!” This outward display of pain is the act of
痛がる.

In Japanese, directly stating someone else’s inner feelings in an assertive way is
unnatural, since you are not supposed to know exactly how another person feels. In
English, it sounds natural to say “my boyfriend wants Japanese friends”, but in
Japanese, saying 彼氏は日本人の友達を欲しいです is unnatural, because “I” cannot directly express what
“my boyfriend” feels inside. Instead, when your boyfriend outwardly expresses the wish
“I want to have Japanese friends”, you would use 〜がる to describe this act of expressing
his feelings: 彼氏は日本人の友達を欲しがっています. 3. よかったです is the past tense (polite form) of the
adjective よい, which literally means “good”, so よかったです literally means “it was good”. In
Japanese, this expression is used when something turns out to be good. For example,
imagine you are worried about something, say, someone you know was missing in the woods,
and you were concerned about him. But then it turns out that he came back safely. In
this situation, since things turned out fine, you would say よかったです (It was good that
things turned out well). In the dialogue, Alice says よかったです because things worked out in
her favor: her boyfriend might gain the Japanese friend he wanted. It’s like saying,
“I’m glad things turned out this way”.

```sentence
マサル：そうなんですか。心の声：彼氏がいるのか。チクショー！
Masaru: I see… (inner voice: She has a boyfriend? Damn it!)
```

## Vocabulary

- はじめまして： “It is the first time (we meet)” (A common greeting phrase when meeting
  someone for the first time.)
- お名前は？: “What’s your name?” (お名前は？ is an incomplete sentence because it has no
  predicate. The full form is お名前は何ですか？, but it’s very common to omit the ending.)
- 僕: “I” (a first-person pronoun typically used by men)
- AはBと言います: “A is called B.” / “My name is B.”
- あなたは？: “And you?” / “How about you?”
- 私: I (the most common first-person pronoun used by both men and women)
- AはBです: “A is B”
- 学生: student
- 〜ですか？: a sentence ending used to ask a question
- はい: yes
- 語学学校: language school
- 〜で勉強しています: “(I’m) studying at ~”
- 働いています: “(I’m) working”
- お国はどちらですか？: “Which country are you from?”
- AはBから来ました: “A came from B”
- イギリス: the UK
- へえ: an interjection used when learning something (like “I see”)
- ロンドン: London
- 〜出身: “from ~” / “originating from ~” (used for where someone comes from)
- いえ: no
- Aじゃなくて、B: “Not A, but B”
- バーミンガム: Birmingham
- そうなんですか: an interjection used when learning something (similar to へえ, “I see”)
- いつ: when
- 日本: Japan
- 来ました: came
- か？: question particle
- 去年: last year
- あのー: a filler word often used before a question or request (like “Um…” or “Well…”)
- 連絡先交換できませんか？: “Can we exchange contact information?”
- いいですよ: “Sure” / “That’s fine”
- LINE: the most common messaging app in Japan
- QRコード: QR code
- 〜を出します: “I’ll show you ~”
- スキャンしました: “I scanned it”
- 今度: “next time”
- いっしょに: together
- 食事しましょう: “Let’s have a meal”
- いいですね: “Sounds good” / “That’s nice”
- 彼氏: boyfriend
- 日本人: Japanese person/people
- 友達: friend
- 〜を欲しがっていた: “(He) wanted (to have) ~”
- 〜ので: because (used to explain a reason)
- よかったです: “That’s great” / “I’m glad”
- 心の声: inner voice
- 彼氏がいるのか: “So, (you) have a boyfriend…” (a realization)
- チクショー: literally “beast/animal,” but used as an exclamation of anger or frustration
  when something doesn’t go one’s way. Roughly, “Damn it!”
